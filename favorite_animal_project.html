Beluga Whales

Belgua whales, also known as white whales, are one of the smallest whale species in the world. They live in coastal Arctic waters, and migrate south every winter when the ocean freezes over. Just like dolphins, belugas communicate with each other in their own language using clicks and whistles. Wanna learn more? Keep reading to become a beluga expert!

Beluga Facts:

  • Belugas are closely related to narwhals
  • Belugas only range from 13-20 feet long, but weight between 1-1.5 tons!
  • Belugas live from 35-50 years
  • Belugas can turn their necks in all directions
  • Belugas are very very slow. They swim at about 4 mph.
